Conduit Fill

Cable overall diameters vs conduit internal diameter.

Fill % = Σ cable CSA ÷ conduit CSA. Common limits: 1 cable 53%, 2 cables 31%, 3+ cables 40%. Verify space factors in AS/NZS 3000 Section 3.

Cable Deratings

Start from the table current-carrying capacity, then apply correction factors.

Typical factors (indicative — confirm against AS/NZS 3008)
  • Ambient: ~1.0 at reference temp; falls as ambient rises (e.g. ~0.88 hotter, lower in roof spaces).
  • Grouping (bunched): 2 ckts ≈ 0.80, 3 ≈ 0.70, 4 ≈ 0.65, 6 ≈ 0.57.
  • Thermal insulation: one side ≈ 0.75; fully surrounded ≈ 0.50.

Derated capacity = base × ambient × grouping × insulation × other. Must stay ≥ design load.

Rules Reference

Quick cards. Always verify against the current standard.

Cable colours (AU)

Single phase: Active brown (was red), Neutral blue (was black), Earth green/yellow.

Three phase: Brown · Black · Grey (was Red · White · Blue).

IP ratings

1st digit (solids): 4 = >1mm wire · 5 = dust protected · 6 = dust tight.

2nd digit (water): 4 = splash · 5 = jets · 6 = strong jets · 7 = immersion.

RCD protection (AS/NZS 3000)

≤30mA RCD required on socket-outlet and lighting final subcircuits in domestic installations. Verify exemptions and circuit grouping limits.

Disconnection times (typical)

Final subcircuits ≤63A (socket-outlets): 0.4s. Distribution / fixed ≤32A: up to 5s. Confirm earthing system and Table values.

Voltage drop limit

Max 5% from point of supply to any point in the installation (AS/NZS 3000 Cl 3.6.2).
